In my thesis I explore some of the different ways that the American post-9/11 climate of fear was cultivated and continues to be perpetuated by the US government using the media. This perpetuation of a national climate of fear is one of the drivers behind the American obsession with what I refer to as zombie culture.I believe one of the methods used to cultivate this national climate of fear was leaders and legislators successfully changing the language used to talk about immigration. Before 9/11 the language used to talk about immigration described an America open to diversity and inclusion. Post-9/11 the language used to talk about immigration described an America needing to protect itself from anyone trying to enter the country legally or illegally as a possible security threat.

With this project I wanted to explore a possible connection between key words recorded in the congressional record for specific years and the output of American zombie films for those same years. The key words I searched for are "immigration and safety" in any single recorded instance. I have searched 5 years pre 9/11 and 5 years post 9/11, in an attempt to compare the pre-9/11 and post-9/11 eras.
